# Real example #2 — Howlin Wolf, 2 May

A second real gig from Sandy's current Apple Notes workflow, pasted verbatim below.

**Read this alongside `real-example.md`.** Several songs (`COMING HOME BABY`, `MOVE ON UP`, `LISTEN HERE`, `SUNNY`, `WATERMELON MAN`, `CANTALOUPE ISLAND`, `MAS QUE NADA`, `BACKLASH`) appear in **both** gigs with **essentially identical song notes** — which validates the brief's core data model: a permanent per-song record + per-gig annotations layered on top. The "compile notes" workflow is currently a copy/paste-and-tweak job; the structured library is exactly what eliminates it.

## What's new vs. real-example.md (extra signals to honour)

- **Document title is venue + date** ("Howlin Wolf - 2nd May") — the gig is named by where and when, not by setlist content.
- **Per-song personnel/soloist annotations** added to titles in this gig: `WATERMELON MAN – Ivan Ian John`, `KELVINGROVESTREET – solos – Ivan Ian Clare SANDY John`, `BACKLASH – Ivan Ian John`. Same songs as the previous gig but with gig-specific soloist lists — exactly the "per-gig annotation, not edit to permanent record" pattern.
- **Inline staging / hardware notes** embedded in titles: `[GUITAR CHANGE]`.
- **Form annotations** at the title line: `SUMMER FIELDS OF JOY – Theme x2 – coda – solos Ivan Ian. Appendix – Ivan Ian`.
- **Minimal entries are valid** — `FOOTPRINTS` is just title + "Piano", no chord info. Some songs don't need detail.
- **History annotations** in patch lines: `Piano (Fm) / Organ (was vibes) P41` — what the patch *used to be* matters to the user.
- **Mode notation** appears: `D Dorian with a minor 6th`, `Bb Dorian (Ab maj)`.
- **Voicing instructions in parentheses**: `(LH E, RH BDF# / LH D, RH ACE)`, `(3 5 7 R)` for intervals from root.
- **Theory shorthand**: `For min11 - R, 7 9 11`.
- **The setlist styling differs from real-example.md** — Set 2 here is ALL CAPS throughout, while Set 1 in `real-example.md` mixed cases to mark optional/TBA. Capitalisation convention may be per-document, not universal.
- **Typos and quirks preserved in the wild** — `Meolody starts no  (note) E`, `DON"T FORGET THE PERCUSSION BREAKS!` (wrong-direction smart quote), `KELVINGROVESTREET` in the body vs. `KELVINGROVE STREET` in the setlist. The library must be lenient about title matching.
- **Band identity is implicit** — the song mix (funk/soul covers + originals like Kelvingrove Street, Fire Eater, Count Da Funk) suggests this and the Big Ed gig are likely the *same band*, despite "covers" vs "originals" being separate categories in the brief. The data model should not assume bands cleanly map to repertoire styles.
